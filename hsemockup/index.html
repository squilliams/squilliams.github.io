Emergency Management

Emergency management is the coordinated set of measures that prepare, respond, and restore operations during incidents that threaten people, assets, or the environment. The goal is simple: protect life first, then stabilize the situation, and enable a safe recovery.

Core Practices for Emergency Readiness

These principles help everyone act quickly and consistently in an emergency. Each card expands on what good looks like in day‑to‑day work.

Communicate Clearly

Provide precise, factual information

When reporting an incident, state what happened, where, when, and who is affected. Share only verified details and avoid speculation to prevent confusion.

Why it matters: clear information accelerates the right response.

Call & Confirm

Contact emergency services and site responders

Know the emergency numbers for your location and confirm whether trained responders are available. Brief the operator to identify a fire, medical, or other emergency.

1. Dial the local emergency number and state your location.
2. Describe the incident type, hazards, and any casualties.
3. Stay on the line until told to hang up.
Know Your Route

Use the shortest route to the assembly area

Familiarize yourself with the nearest assembly point and evacuation map for your work area. If visiting, ask the host for the site plan.

Practice makes this second nature during a real event.

Equipment Ready

Ensure fire and first‑aid equipment is available

Locate extinguishers, hose reels, hydrants, and first‑aid kits. Ensure seals are intact, pressure gauges are in the green, and access is unobstructed.

Plan Awareness

Know and follow the site emergency plan

Before starting any activity, review the emergency plan and identify your escape routes and communication channels for the area.

Keep Routes Clear

Maintain clean, signed, and unobstructed exits

Never store materials in transit corridors. Keep doors unlocked where required and ensure exit signage and lighting are functional.

Personal Readiness Checklist

Immediate Actions

Assess the scene from a safe distance. Do not take unnecessary risk.
Alert emergency services and the site team. Share clear, concise facts.
Assist only if trained and it is safe to do so.
Assemble at the designated point and await instructions.
